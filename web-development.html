Web Development is our forte at Assure Studios; we make sure your website is fast, mobile-friendly, accessible, and secure.Here is an explanation of the four factors in a little more detail.

Security

Your website should never be an HTTP site your website should employ an HTTPS certificate.

HTTP is an abbreviation that stands for Hypertext Transfer Protocol. HTTPS is an abbreviation that stands for Hypertext Transfer Protocol Secure.

The only difference between the two is HTTPS uses TLS (SSL) to encrypt normal HTTP requests and responses. As a result, HTTPS is far more secure than HTTP.

The HTTP protocol directly affects SEO because, In 2015, Google announced that HTTPS websites would rank higher than HTTP websites in Search Engine results pages.

Accessibility

Accessibility is the practice of making your websites usable by as many people as possible, this mainly refers to people with disabilities that cannot navigate a standard website with a mouse or keyboard. .

There should be no barriers that prevent people who experience varying degrees of visual, auditory, cognitive, speech, and physical disabilities to access and interact with your website.

The benefits of accessibility are building a broader customer base, and increasing brand loyalty through inclusion.

mobile friendly web design

Mobile-friendly means that your website performs well and looks goodon mobile devices such as cell phones and tablets. The main objective is for users to have a user experience that is equal to if not better than when someone uses a desktop computer.

The reason mobile-friendly is so vital is that everyone have a mobile device, mobile internet has grown 504% in daily media consumption since 2011. if your website is not mobile-friendly you are losing customers and its hurting your business.

If you need to check if your existing website google has free mobile test at https://search.google.com/test/mobile-friendly

Page Speed

Page speed affects SEO Page speed is a direct ranking factor. It also affects the user experience Studies show how users expect pages to load within two seconds, and after three seconds, up to 40% of users will abandon your site.
